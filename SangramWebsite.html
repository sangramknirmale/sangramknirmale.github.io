About

I am a research scientist at the Centre for infrastructure, Sustainable Transportation and Urban Planning (CiSTUP) at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru. My research interests lie in the mathematical modelling of human choice behaviour in transportation systems, and I specialize in modelling driver behaviour using a discrete-continuous econometric framework and statistical methods.

In my recent research, I have developed a multi-vehicle anticipation-based modelling framework to model the longitudinal movement of drivers. I have also developed models to accommodate errors in drivers' perception of traffic environment variables and to model drivers' intents and two-dimensional movements. To simulate the heterogeneous disorderly traffic streams, I have built a simulation platform using the proposed driver behaviour models.

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering from the Government College of Engineering Karad, an M-Tech in Urban Planning from Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology (VNIT), and a PhD in Civil Engineering from Indian Institute of Science (IISc). Throughout my academic journey, I have consistently demonstrated a keen interest in research and have made significant contributions to my field of study. I have published four research articles in reputed international journals, including Transportation Research Part B, Transportation Research Part C, and Transportation Research Part A, and presented more than ten papers at renowned conferences such as Transportation Research Board (TRB) annual meeting, International Association for Travel Behaviour Research (IATBR) and International Choice Modelling Conference (ICMC).
